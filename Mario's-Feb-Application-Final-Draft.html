Allow me to introduce myself.

The abridged version is that I'm a 25 year old Californian with a Brazilian family, living in Canada and eager to see more of the world.

I was born in Toronto to Brazilian parents, and though most of my family is from Fortaleza in the north of the country (known for forro music and white sand beaches), I spent my young childhood just outside of São Paolo, before moving briefly to the Spanish basque country and finally to San Diego, California. Upon finishing high school I opted not for a university in the United States but in my birth country, Canada. McGill is one of two anglophone universities in Montréal, Québec - a very unique bilingual city where the rest of my story (so far) takes place.

What seems like a less straightforward path in life applies to my career choice as well.

Though I started a bachelor's in Civil Engineering, after taking a computer science course I quickly found that my interest lies in programming, especially within an educational environment. Throughout my degree I found opportunities to teach others the basics of coding - through a Teaching Assistant position, my own tutoring business, and a summer tech camp - and took on side projects of my own to learn more about web development and hone my skills. With this experience I have been able to start breaking into the world of programming without a degree in Computer Science, and find experience in a field that truly inspires me. I honestly feel this to be my greatest achievement so far, and am excited to delve deeper.

Another challenge awaits in London...

My partner was recently given the opportunity to transfer to her studio's London headquarters, where she'll help create visual effects for movies. Since we had been looking for a chance to develop our careers and see a bit more of the world, deciding to make the move was easy; we'll be in London for a few years, and I can't wait to explore everything this city has to offer. The hard part is pursuing my passion for programming within a new framework - a challenge I'm eager to overcome.
