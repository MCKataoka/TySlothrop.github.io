Welcome!

Your guide for climbing in Montréal, Québec.

Whether you're looking for bouldering or sport climbing, indoor or outdoor problems, a high-intensity workout or relaxed gym experience - the city of Montréal and southern Québec offer a wide array of options to get you on (and up) the wall.

Curious first timer or seasoned veteran, there is a Montréal gym to fit your bill.

If you're more the outdoorsy type, though, there are also sites just outside of the city that will bring you and your gear a bit closer to the Canadian wilderness.

INDOOR GYMS

Shakti Rock Gym

My personal favourite, Shakti is located in Montréal's trendy Mile End neighbourhood and offers a bouldering experience with a mom & pop feel. With their team of experienced setters, they change their problems about once a month, and you can always expect to find challenging routes for any level of climber. All this with a laid-back, local feel and always the perfect soundtrack.

Learn More

Zero Gravity

Located in an old theatre in the heart of Montréal's historic Plateau neighbourhood, Zero Gravity provides both bouldering and rope climbing in a beautiful setting. Yoga classes are included in the membership, so you're able to stretch it out between climbing sessions.

Learn More

Allez Up!

Montréal's most popular climbing gym, this re-purposed factory in quartier Saint Henri is reknowned for hosting some of North America's most creative setters, all the while providing a fantastic view of the Canal LaChine as you make your way up an old grain silo. With its massive walls and challenging problems, this is one gym you don't want to miss.

Learn More

Bloc Shop

A bouldering gym with two locations in Parc Extension and Hochelaga neighbourhoods, Bloc Shop boasts the largest facilities in the city, complete with climbing workshops, summer camps, and even live music events in the evenings. One visit alone will convince you of its Québécois coolness.
